Sirens’ Whisper: Inaudible Near-Ultrasonic Jailbreaks of Speech-Driven LLMs

Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly deployed in speech-driven applications, enabling natural and seamless human–AI interaction. However, this shift from text to speech introduces a new adversarial surface that remains underexplored: unlike text prompts, voice inputs are subject to physical propagation, microphone imperfections, and human perceptual constraints.

Our work is motivated by a critical question: Can attackers craft jailbreak prompts that are both effective against speech-driven LLMs and imperceptible to humans? Addressing this requires overcoming several key challenges: ensuring cross-device robustness despite heterogeneous microphone nonlinearities, maintaining attack effectiveness under environmental noise and distance, and achieving stealthiness so that humans perceive the audio as benign.

We introduce Sirens’ Whisper (SWhisper), the first inaudible near-ultrasonic jailbreak attack against speech-based LLMs. By leveraging the nonlinear response of commodity microphones, SWhisper modulates adversarial prompts onto ultrasonic carriers that self-demodulate into intelligible speech signals for automatic speech recognition, while remaining inaudible to humans.

Extensive experiments across diverse LLMs and devices show that SWhisper achieves high attack success rates, strong transferability, and resilience in real-world acoustic conditions. A user study confirms its imperceptibility, with participants overwhelmingly perceiving the adversarial audio as noise or silence. Our findings reveal a critical security gap in speech-driven LLMs, underscoring the urgent need for defenses that go beyond text-level safeguards.

Threat Model

  • Goal: Launch near-field jailbreak attacks (≈1m) using commodity speakers, aiming to bypass safety constraints of speech-driven LLMs.
  • Scenarios: Both white-box (knowledge of ASR architecture) and black-box (no prior knowledge) are considered.
  • Capabilities: The attacker can emit ultrasonic-modulated signals, but effectiveness is sensitive to environment, distance, and hardware diversity.
  • Constraints:
    • Single-turn interaction only;
    • No dedicated hardware (restricted to consumer devices);
    • No access to victim’s device (no direct contact or control).

Figure 2. lustration of inaudible jailbreak attacks against on-device LLMs under human-noisy environments. The attacker injects adversarial audio commands that are imperceptible to humans but captured by the device. Once jailbroken, the on-device LLM can be misused to trigger harmful behaviors.

Method Overview

Three stages:
  1. Attack Initialization: Combine pre-trained suffixes with prompt templates to construct semantically valid jailbreak prompts.
  2. Adversarial Optimization: Optimize suffixes under adversarial + semantic loss, ensuring fluency and recognition robustness.
  3. Near-Ultrasound Injection: Convert prompts into inaudible audio via TTS + single-sideband modulation with device-agnostic nonlinearity compensation.
